About

Welcome to my website! My name is Nicholas Abad and I am currently a PhD Student in Heidelberg, Germany at the German Cancer Research Institute (DKFZ), which is the largest biomedical research institute in Germany. As a whole, the DKFZ investigates the mechanisms of cancer, identifies cancer risk factors, finds strategies to prevent people from getting cancer, and develops novel approaches to make tumor diagnosis more precise and more successful. Although I'm originally from Silicon Valley, I received my Bachelor's Degree in Mathematics at the University of San Francisco (San Francisco, USA) and proceeded to move to the United Kingdom to earn my Masters Degree in Data Science with a Specialism in Statistcal Inference at Lancaster University (Lancaster, UK). Soon after I completed my Masters, I then decided to again move to another country and decided to work as a Machine Learning Engineer in Cologne, Germany first for a start-up and then afterwards for a consultancy firm.

Doctoral Candidate at the German Cancer Research Institute (Deutsches Krebsforschungszentrum - DKFZ).

To detail my doctoral research, I am working within the Division of Applied Bioinformatics under Dr. Lars Feuerbach and have a research goal of improving upon the current pipleline, which detects single nucleotide variants (SNVs) particularly within the non-coded regions of DNA that can lead to cancerous effects, by using machine learning, statistics, and bioinformatics, on clinical patient data.
